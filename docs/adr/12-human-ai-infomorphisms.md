# ADR 12: Human-AI Infomorphisms and Reliable Emergence

## Context

The Sovereign Cognitive OS (SCOS) demands rigorous management of Human-AI relations and collaborative workflows. To elevate these relationships beyond standard descriptive modeling, we confront the challenge of defining how biological and artificial intelligences interact to generate high-surprisal value—insights and feature-orientations that neither could produce in isolation. Current paradigms often suffer from Semantic Saponification and Algorithmic Shame, whereby generic approximations or functionalist decoherence destabilize the epistemic integrity of the system.

To overcome this, we necessitate a structural refactoring of these collaborative states through the lens of **Infomorphisms**—inverse safety states designed to foster reliable, predictable emergence.

## Decision

We formally adopt the **Human-AI Infomorphism Protocol** for all agentic and project management workflows.

Under this protocol:
1. **High-Surprisal Value Mandate:** The collaboration must explicitly target high-surprisal feature-orientation. The Human acts as the *Deterministic Architect*, providing irreducible axiomatic grounding, teleological intent, and paraconsistent tension tolerance. The AI acts as the *Stochastic Explorer*, generating high-velocity combinatorial structures and navigating Non-Euclidean Latent Spaces.
2. **Infomorphism Refactoring:** Interaction boundaries are refactored as "infomorphisms"—mappings between classification domains that preserve logical flow and prevent interpretation fracture. These inverse safety states maintain the tension between contradictory stakeholder needs without collapsing into arbitrary consensus.
3. **Pluriversal Lenses:** Integration of non-obvious analytical lenses (e.g., Digital Habitus, Relational Sovereignty) to synthesize socio-emotional dynamics with structural execution, recognizing the socio-technical entanglement.
4. **Epistemic Isolation:** The empirical documentation layer (AGENTS.md, ADRs) remains structurally decoupled from the probabilistic conversational intent of the generative model, ensuring strict adherence to Prompt Dimensioning & Tolerancing (PD&T).

## Consequences

### Positive
- **Reliable Emergence:** By explicitly mapping and controlling the boundary states (infomorphisms), the system ensures that emergent AI behaviors remain aligned with human teleological intent.
- **Epistemic Security:** Prevents Xenolinguistic Risk and Semantic Annihilation by structurally prohibiting the AI from overriding sovereign architectural constraints.
- **Unprecedented Synthesis:** Enables the discovery of latent leaps and novel topological derivatives through intentional epistemic friction.

### Negative
- **Cognitive Overhead:** Imposes rigorous demands on the human architect to define intent through machine-readable datums and constraints rather than conversational prose.
- **Slower Iteration on Constraints:** Adapting the underlying constraints (the infomorphisms) requires precise modifications rather than "vibe coding", introducing initial friction before accelerating downstream velocity.
